Lloyd McQueen Petite Sirah 2013

Description

When Irish dairy farmer Lloyd McQueen immigrated to California in the 1930s, he didn’t expect to join the region’s burgeoning wine industry. But he quickly found his niche growing Carignane and Zinfandel grapes in the heart of Suisun Valley. These viticultural roots are strong, as McQueen’s grandson and great-grandson, Howard and Brian Babcock, currently farm his Morrison Lane Vineyard, home to this bold, earthy Petite Sirah.

Fun Fact

Ninety percent of the Petite Sirah grown in the U.S. is actually a French blending grape called Durif, which is now rarely found in its native country.
